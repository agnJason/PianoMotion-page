Abstract

Recent years have witnessed the increasing importance of artificial intelligence in the field of education, and designing music instrument instructing systems remains an open problem. In this work, we introduce a piano-hand motion prediction benchmark for guidance on hand movements and techniques for piano playing. We provide a annotated dataset, PianoHand12M consisting of 131 hours videos of piano playing from an overhead perspective with 12 million annotated hand poses. We also introduce a powerful baseline models which generates hand motions with piano audios through a position predictor and a position-guide gesture generator. Furthermore, several evaluation metrics are designed to assess the performance of baseline models, including motion similarity, smoothness, positional accuracy of left and right hand, and overall similarity of movement distribution. While piano key presses based on sheet music or audios are easily accessible, PianoHand12M aims to provide guidance on piano fingering for piano instruction.
